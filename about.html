About This Project

A student-led project focused on pediatric burn recovery: what happens after the initial injury, after the hospital, and throughout the months and years of healing that follow.

More Than a Scar is a student-led project focused on pediatric burn recovery: what happens after the initial injury, after the hospital, and throughout the months and years of healing that follow.

I started this project because I experienced a significant scald burn as a child. The injury required hospitalization, long-term bandage care, follow-up appointments, and years of scar management and laser treatments. Although I recovered well, the experience stayed with me, not only because of the physical pain, but because of how confusing and isolating recovery could feel.

As I became interested in medicine, I began thinking more about the experiences of other children and families affected by burns. Excellent medical resources already exist, but families may still struggle to find practical guidance, emotional reassurance, and stories from people who have been through something similar.

This project is my attempt to help bridge that gap by bringing together survivor stories, trusted resources, and perspectives from families and healthcare professionals.

Founder’s Note

This project is still growing. My hope is to build it slowly and thoughtfully, learning from burn survivors, families, clinicians, and organizations already doing important work in this space.

More Than a Scar is an educational project focused on pediatric burn recovery. Content is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for medical advice.
